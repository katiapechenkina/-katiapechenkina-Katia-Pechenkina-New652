Hello, I'm Katia Pechenkina.

I am a graduate student journalist at The Philip Merrill College of Journalism at The University of Maryland.


About me

I have experience in writing for a college newspaper, anchoring, producing and editing video for a student-produced weekly news show.
I was also a part of Democracy and the Informed citizen emerging journalist fellowship program. This fall I am writing about health in Washington D.C. area.


Experience:
Journalism Fellow
Democracy and the Informed citizen emerging journalist fellowship program, California humanities
February 2021 – June 2021

With a team of three other fellows reported on the disproportionate impact of COVID-19 on National City’s smallest Latinx-owned businesses. As a team we produced a multimedia project that brought together an enterprise story with interactives and data visualizations, five small business profiles, a podcast featuring the mayor, a video story and photo gallery.

Multimedia Journalist
Newscene, San Diego City College
February 2021 – May 2021

I was a weekly contributor to the student-produced TV news broadcast, where I alternated between different broadcasting positions including anchoring, producing, editing and writing. I pitched 2-5 stories a week and wrote 2-3 scripts per show in broadcast style on deadline. I also took part in promoting the show content on social media (Twitter, Instagram, YouTube, Facebook).

News Editor
City Times, San Diego City College
August 2020 – May 2021

I was a part of a 25-student staff producing news content five days a week for the digitally-driven student news website serving the 16,000-student campus of City College. Led coverage of the impact of COVID-19 on the Latino community in San Diego County, localizing the story with as interview of a campus health official and data specific to the campus, a Hispanic Serving Institution. I also covered local elections in November 2020, and focused on the competitive race for the 50th District on Election Day.
